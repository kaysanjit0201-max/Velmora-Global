Operational Flow

The Repatriation Process

How Velmora Global International handles transit logistics from the moment of dispatch to final handoff.

1

Immediate Registration

We register the case immediately upon receiving notification. Our 24/7 legal team secures authorization letters from the family and initiates contacts with local authorities.

2

Sanitary Preparation

Certified medical practitioners perform embalming. The body is placed within a hermetically sealed zinc/lead-lined wooden coffin, meeting IATA standards.

3

Custom Clearances

We dispatch legal liaisons to local foreign offices or embassies to cancel passports, secure NOC certifications, and clear infectious disease declarations.

4

Customs and Flight Booking

Air cargo space is booked. Airport Health Organization (APHO) clearance is obtained at the cargo terminals, and customs clearance is cleared by our agents.

5

Handoff to Next of Kin

Upon landing at the destination cargo terminal, our local representative clears customs and transfers the remains directly to the family or receiving funeral home.

FAQ Desk

Frequently Asked Questions

Essential information about legal, financial, and logistical aspects of deceased repatriation.

Typically, international repatriation to India takes between 2 to 5 days. This depends heavily on securing the local Death Certificate, getting the NOC and Passport Cancellation from the Indian Embassy in the host country, and flight cargo bookings.

The eCARe (e-Clearance for Afterlife Remains) portal is a platform set up by the Ministry of Health, Government of India, to speed up clearances for importing human remains. Next of kin or authorized representatives must submit clearances there. Velmora Global International handles all eCARe uploads and registrations on your behalf.

Yes, we coordinate the transfer of ashes. This requires a Cremation Certificate, an Hermetic/Airtight urn certificate, and clearance stamps from the departure and receiving customs units. The process is faster and more cost-effective than repatriating physical remains.

A hermetically sealed casket is an airtight and waterproof container, usually lined with zinc or lead, designed to prevent the escape of gases or bacteria. International airline cargo rules and WHO guidelines strictly require hermetic sealing for the transport of human remains.
