Process Overview

Indian Embassy Clearance & APHO Clearance

Repatriating a deceased loved one back to India is a sensitive, time-critical task regulated under international air standards and the Indian Ministry of Health and Family Welfare. Inbound remains must undergo clearance by Airport Health Officers (APHO) at primary entry ports (such as New Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Chennai).

Velmora Global International coordinates directly with local funeral directors in the country of passing, cancels the deceased's passport at the nearest Indian Consulate to obtain a Death NOC, registers the documentation on the online eCARe portal, and clears customs immediately upon landing in India.

Embassy Clearances

Aviation Packaging Standards

Before boarding caskets on flights, airlines require hermetic seals and custom outer wooden boxes.

WHO Health Seals

Embalming certificates issued by registered doctors in compliance with WHO quarantine instructions.

Hermetic Zinc Liner

Caskets must be sealed airtight (hermetically sealed) to prevent pressure leaks inside planes.

Consular Stamps NOC

Embassy passport cancellations and death NOC stamps managed by daily runner dispatches.
