Process Overview

Indian Embassies & Foreign Consulates Cleared

Consular clearance is the most complex administrative hurdle in deceased remains shipping. Consulates require a complete, original set of certificates—local death records, embalming logs, coroner toxicology results, and police clearances—to issue a No Objection Certificate (NOC) and cancel the deceased's passport. If cause of death is not certified, or if any document lacks translation, the consulate will reject the case, causing costly airline cargo delays.

Velmora Global International maintains specialized liaison coordinators in New Delhi. We dispatch runners daily to foreign embassies (including the US, UK, UAE, and Australian diplomatic missions) and Ministry of External Affairs offices to secure stamp validations, notary clearances, and apostilles without delay.
